Assistive device for old age

(A Smart Door Bell)

Using IR Receive and one old remote control

Calling Bells are common and used in almost every house in our society. As soon as the bell rings we come to know someone has come but very often it happens that we are busy with our household work or due to some another reason, we cannot open the door immediately. This answering bell has a solution to this problem.

Problem Statement

The problem came up when respected Chairperson of our school Mrs. Bhanumati Neelkanthan spoke to us. She is an old lady who celebrated her 80th birthday recently.

Chairpersons Comment “Children, when there is a knock at my door, I am unable to open the door immediately to attend my guest, because sometimes I am busy in work and sometimes I am unable to move fast since I have pain in my legs. Can you make a device with the help of which I can answer the person waiting?  The answer such as ‘wait for five minutes’ or ‘I am busy’ etc is displayed on the screen." she added “this will not only be helpful to me but to a lot of people who are in old age”

This statement was enough for me to start thing and I started discussing the problem with my friends, family members, and aged people around us in my society. I was surprised to know that this problem was faced by a lot of people........

I defined the problem as “To make a device which can answer our guest knocking the door if we are unable to open the door after the bell ring”

Solution

DESIGN CYCLE:

I: EMPATHIZE

  • Problem in old age to move fast
  • Problem in answering people from washroom
  • Problem of opening the door when busy

II. DEFINE

To make a device which can answer our guest knocking the door if we are unable to open the door after the bell ring........

III. IDEATE

        Brain Storming

  • A doorbell made using AI
  • Make an app to communicate with the guests
  • Send SMS from mobile
  • Make an device to display the message to the guest in the door with remote and IR Receiver

Idea chosen after brain storming

Make an device to display the message to the guest in the door with remote and IR Receiver

Pre-requisites

  • Basic knowledge of Electronics circuits
  • Arduino programming.

Material Required

  • IR Receiver  
  • One Remote Control
  • Arduino UNO
  • LCD display
  • Wires
  • Breadboard
  • Wi-Fi Module
  • Camera module
  • 7” Display Screen (now using 1.8” TFT)
  • 2 pairs of microphone and speaker
  • Android Mobile Phone

IV. PROTOTYPE:

(The first working prototype)

Block Diagram

V. Working:

The first prototype was equipped only with IR receiver and LCD. On pressing any button on the remote, text would be displayed on the LCD.

Actually, pressing any button on the remote would transmit hex codes, which are unique for each button on the remote. Using the Arduino IDE (software) and a basic circuit, I determined the hex codes of different buttons and used these hex codes to display text on the screen.

The second prototype not only has IR receiver but a Wi-Fi module as well, along with the LCD screen. Text can not only be sent using remote but any android Smartphone connected to a Wi-Fi network. This will enable the user to send custom messages to the screen.

VI. Test:

After testing, the device was approved by our respected chairperson, Mrs. Bhanumati Neelkanthan to work properly and also commented that it was “a perfect solution to her problem”.

Now working.............

Now I am working to attach a camera to the prototype so that the person can view his guest on the screen. This will be a great feature as it will not only allow the person to know his guest but also to keep an eye around. (Now, the camera displays the scene on a TFT 1.8” screen)

Planning Ahead:

Till now, I’ve connected IR and Wi-Fi to the same board and now I look ahead to merge the screen and custom text app into one. Using this, user will be able to send the message to the LCD without looking on the screen. Also I wish to add a pair of speakers and microphones to the product. This will cause ease in communication between the guest and the user, without the fear of leaking the phone-number to anyone.
